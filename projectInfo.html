Project Info

Expected Visualizations

  1. Overall Changes in Food Security in Adults between 2022 and 2025
  2. Having Coping Behaviors (Fasting, Skipping Meals, Eating Less) Become More or Less Common
  3. Has the Duration of Receiving Food Security Benefits Increased or Decreased

To many Americans in the United States, getting access to food is an increasing issue with food insecurity plaguing millions. In recent years, there has been a dramatic increase in the number of Americans facing this new public health crisis. Food insecurity has a correlation with future health concerns, making it a pressing issue in today's environment. The current food stamp program offers a reprieve of this issue allowing people to get food for themselves and their families. The information provided by the Center for Disease Control and Prevention allows individuals to view the effect on food insecurity and food stamps on the American public.
